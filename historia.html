History and Culture

San Jorge is a municipality in the department of Rivas, located on the shores of Lake Cocibolca in southwestern Nicaragua. It is mainly known for being the departure port to Ometepe Island, making it a key point for national and international tourism. Its proximity to the city of Rivas and its access to the lake give it a strategic position both commercially and touristically. This municipality has an economy based on artisanal fishing, agriculture (such as plantain and cassava cultivation), and in recent years there has been growing interest in community and rural tourism. San Jorge also has attractions such as its boardwalk, from where you can enjoy beautiful views of the lake and the volcanoes of Ometepe.

Origin of the name

One version indicates that between 1550-1560 Fray Pedro de Betanzos founded the Franciscan convent of San Jorge. Another tradition says that on April 23, 1777, an image of San Jorge appeared on the shores of the lake, in Nahualapa.
